🔬 What Is BPC-157?

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound 157) is a synthetic pentadecapeptide derived from a protein found naturally in human gastric juice. It consists of 15 amino acids (sequence: Gly-Glu-Pro-Pro-Pro-Gly-Lys-Pro-Ala-Asp-Asp-Ala-Gly-Leu-Val) and is one of the most extensively studied regenerative research peptides in existence. The compound was first described in the 1990s by Dr. Predrag Sikiric and colleagues at the University of Zagreb, who isolated a peptide fragment from human gastric juice that appeared to have extraordinary tissue-protective properties in rodent models. Since then, over 150 papers have been published exploring its effects on tendons, ligaments, muscle, bone, gut tissue, neurological function, and vascular systems.

📉 Why Do BPC-157 Prices Vary So Much?

BPC-157 is a relatively short peptide (15 amino acids) making it one of the more straightforward peptides to synthesize reliably. This is why it tends to be among the more affordable research compounds. Price variation stems from: **Testing stringency**: BPC-157 is a commodity peptide in the research space — vendors compete heavily on price, and some cut corners on testing. The difference between a $35 and $65 vial often comes down to whether third-party HPLC testing was conducted. **Lyophilization quality**: Proper freeze-drying preserves peptide integrity during storage. Substandard lyophilization can accelerate degradation, meaning a cheaper vial may be less effective as research material. **Vial size**: Larger vials (10mg vs 5mg) offer better $/mg economics at most vendors.

✅ How to Choose a BPC-157 Vendor — Quality Checklist

BPC-157 purchasing checklist: **COA verification**: At minimum, look for HPLC purity data showing ≥98% and a completed-date within the past 12 months. Given how often COAs are copied or faked in this market, consider vendors who post batch-specific (not product-general) COA documentation. **Peptide sequence confirmation**: Some vendors sell "BPC-157 Acetate" (with an acetate counterion) vs "BPC-157 free acid." For most research applications, either form works, but be aware of the distinction when calculating doses by mass. **Storage instructions**: Lyophilized BPC-157 stores well at -20°C for 2+ years. Reconstituted, it should be refrigerated and used within 4 weeks. Quality vendors communicate this clearly.

⚖️ BPC-157 vs Other Peptides — What's the Difference?

**BPC-157 vs TB-500** Both are heavily studied recovery and regenerative peptides but with distinct mechanisms. BPC-157 appears to work primarily through the NO (nitric oxide) and VEGF (vascular endothelial growth factor) pathways, while TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4) regulates actin polymerization and cell migration. Many researchers combine them — the BPC-157 + TB-500 Blend is available from most vendors, often at a better combined $/mg than buying separately. **Oral vs Injectable BPC-157** Unlike most peptides, BPC-157 has shown activity in rodent models when administered orally, likely due to its gastric origin and resistance to proteolytic degradation. Most research compounds are sold as injectable lyophilized powder, but some vendors offer oral BPC-157 capsules.
