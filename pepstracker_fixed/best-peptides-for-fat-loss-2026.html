The four mechanisms, briefly

Metabolic peptides differ by how many receptor pathways they engage. In broad terms, triple agonism (Retatrutide) engages more pathways than dual agonism (Tirzepatide), which engages more than single agonism (Semaglutide). Amylin analogs (Cagrilintide) hit a separate appetite pathway (the CALCR/RAMP amylin receptor complex) and are often studied in combination with a GLP-1 for additive rather than competing effects — the basis of CagriSema.

Frequently asked questions

Which peptide has the strongest fat-loss data in 2026?

In published research, Retatrutide (a triple GLP-1/GIP/glucagon agonist) has reported the largest mean weight-reduction figures in its Phase 3 TRIUMPH program, ahead of Tirzepatide and Semaglutide. It remains investigational.

What is CagriSema?

CagriSema is a combination of Cagrilintide (an amylin analog) and Semaglutide (a GLP-1 agonist). The pairing engages two different appetite pathways and reported roughly 20% weight reduction in the REDEFINE 1 trial.
