Semaglutide vs Tirzepatide — Key Differences

Mechanism

Semaglutide is a GLP-1 receptor agonist — it mimics glucagon-like peptide 1. Tirzepatide is a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist, acting on both glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide and GLP-1 receptors simultaneously. This dual action is why Tirzepatide is generally considered the more potent compound in clinical research.

Price

Semaglutide is consistently cheaper across all vendors — typically by 30–50% per mg. For budget-conscious research, Semaglutide offers more milligrams per dollar.
