What is a peptide?

A peptide is a short chain of amino acids — the same building blocks that make up proteins, just fewer of them. Amino acids link together with peptide bonds; a handful of them strung together is a peptide, and a long chain (roughly 50+) folded into a 3-D shape is a protein. So the simplest definition: peptides are small proteins, short enough to act as precise signaling molecules.

Why peptides are useful

Because they're small and specific, peptides act like keys that fit particular locks (receptors). A peptide can switch a single biological pathway on or off with precision — which is why so many are studied as signaling molecules for metabolism, tissue repair, growth, and the nervous system.

The main categories of research peptides

  • Metabolic / GLP-1 & GIP — Semaglutide, Tirzepatide, Retatrutide, Cagrilintide. Studied for appetite and metabolic pathways. (See the fat-loss guide.)
  • Healing & recovery — BPC-157, TB-500, GHK-Cu, KPV. Tissue repair and anti-inflammatory research. (See BPC-157 + TB-500.)
  • Growth hormone secretagogues — CJC-1295, Ipamorelin, Tesamorelin, Sermorelin. Studied for pulsatile GH release.
  • Cognitive / nootropic — Semax, Selank. Focus, mood and neuroprotection research.
  • Longevity & other — Epithalon, NAD+, MOTS-c, SS-31. Cellular aging and mitochondrial research.

Frequently asked questions

What is a peptide in simple terms?

A peptide is a short chain of amino acids — essentially a small protein. Amino acids link together with peptide bonds; a short chain is a peptide, and a long folded chain is a protein.

What is the difference between a peptide and a protein?

Size. Peptides are short chains of roughly 2–50 amino acids, while proteins are longer chains (about 50 or more) folded into a 3-D structure. Peptides act as precise signaling molecules.

What are peptides used for in research?

Research peptides are studied across metabolism (GLP-1 compounds), tissue repair (BPC-157, TB-500), growth hormone release (CJC-1295, Ipamorelin), cognition (Semax, Selank) and longevity (Epithalon, NAD+). They are sold for laboratory research use only.
