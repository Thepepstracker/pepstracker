About Research-Grade Vitamin B12

Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin) is an essential water-soluble vitamin with roles in DNA synthesis, red blood cell formation, and neurological function. Research peptide vendors primarily sell the Cyanocobalamin form — the most stable synthetic version — as a lyophilized powder or injectable solution for laboratory use.

What's the difference between Cyanocobalamin and Methylcobalamin?

Cyanocobalamin is the synthetic, most chemically stable form of B12 — preferred for research use. Methylcobalamin is the active, naturally occurring form. Ion Peptides carries both. Mile High Compounds stocks only Cyanocobalamin.
